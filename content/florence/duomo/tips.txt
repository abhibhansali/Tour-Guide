The combined "Brunelleschi Pass" gets you the dome climb, the Baptistery, the bell tower, the cathedral museum, and the crypt for around thirty euros. Book online — dome times sell out days in advance.
The cathedral itself is free to enter, but the line moves slowly. First entry of the day is the calmest.
The dome climb is four hundred and sixty-three steps with no elevator, in narrow tilted stairs between the two shells. Not suitable for severe claustrophobia or knee problems. The Campanile next door — Giotto's bell tower — has a similar number of steps but they're wider and less claustrophobic, and the view from the top is just as good.
The Baptistery is small, intense, and easy to overlook in a quick visit. It's worth the twenty minutes for the mosaic ceiling alone.
The Museo dell'Opera del Duomo, across from the cathedral, houses the original Ghiberti panels, the original Donatello sculptures from the bell tower, and Michelangelo's late Pietà. It's the under-visited gem of the whole complex.
Strict dress code applies inside the cathedral — shoulders and knees covered. No photos with flash. Inside the Baptistery, no photos at all.
The piazza is full of "Italian leather" shops that are tourist traps. Buy leather in the San Lorenzo market a few blocks north, where the prices are real.
There's an excellent gelateria called Edoardo on the side street west of the cathedral. Ignore the ones directly on the piazza.
Bathrooms aren't inside the cathedral. Use the cafes around the piazza or the museum restroom.
The Duomo lights up beautifully at night. The piazza is much emptier after nine.
