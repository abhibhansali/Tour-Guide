Book online days in advance. The Accademia queue can be over two hours in peak season.
First entry of the day, around 8:15, is dramatically calmer than later slots.
Plan ninety minutes. The David deserves twenty; the rest of the museum can be done in an hour.
Photography is allowed, no flash. The David is the most photographed sculpture in the world — there's no need to add to it. Look at him in person instead.
The David tribune has benches along the perimeter. Sit and watch. Most visitors don't take this time.
Don't miss the unfinished Prisoners along the corridor approaching the David. Many visitors march past them without realizing what they are.
The museum of historical musical instruments upstairs is included with the ticket and almost always empty. Worth twenty minutes.
The bathrooms are limited — use them before entering the David tribune.
No large bags allowed; cloakroom at the entrance.
The streets around the museum are full of cafes targeting the queue crowd. Walk one block away for better and cheaper.
The Galleria dell'Accademia and the Uffizi are different museums and need separate tickets. They're not connected.
