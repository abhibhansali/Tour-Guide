Entry is ticketed now — around five euros for adults. Still free on the first Sunday of the month and for E.U. residents under eighteen.
Mornings before ten are the quietest. By lunchtime the porch is packed.
It's a working church — Mass is said most evenings around six-thirty, and the building closes to tourists during services.
Modest dress is appreciated but rarely enforced. Shoulders covered is more than enough.
Photos are allowed; flash is discouraged but no one polices it. Tripods need a permit.
The marble floor is slightly convex — easy to miss, easy to trip on. Watch your feet near the center.
Public bathrooms are not inside. The cafés on Piazza della Rotonda are your best bet — buy a coffee, it's twenty minutes of seating.
The gelato at Giolitti, three blocks east, is worth the small detour.
Pickpockets work the square at peak hours. Front pocket only for phones and wallets.
