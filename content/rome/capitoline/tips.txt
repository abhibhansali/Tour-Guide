Entry is about sixteen euros and covers both palaces. Book online to avoid the entrance line.
The two palaces are connected underground; you can move between them without going outside.
Plan three hours for a thorough visit; ninety minutes for one palace.
Open daily; last entry one hour before closing.
The piazza outside is free and worth a separate visit at sunset. The view westward from the back of the Palazzo Senatorio terrace, over the Forum, is one of the best in Rome.
Photography is allowed without flash.
The cafe inside the Palazzo dei Conservatori has the best view of the Forum from inside any museum. The food is overpriced but the location is unbeatable.
Free entry on the first Sunday of each month for foreigners and on a few other days throughout the year.
The Vittoriano monument — the giant white-marble palace below the Capitoline — is also worth visiting. Free elevator to the top for a panoramic view; some Italians despise the building but the view is excellent.
The Roman Forum is just below the Capitoline. The Capitoline ticket includes a viewing terrace looking down into the Forum but doesn't include Forum entry. Plan separate tickets.
The walk down the Cordonata back toward Piazza Venezia is one of the best architectural transitions in the city.
