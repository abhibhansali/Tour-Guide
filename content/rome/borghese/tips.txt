Book weeks in advance. Same-day tickets are rarely available; even months ahead the popular slots fill quickly.
Entry is by two-hour timed window — you must arrive in your slot and exit at the end. Plan your day around it.
Entry is about thirteen euros plus a small reservation fee. The reservation fee is mandatory.
The gallery is at the eastern edge of the Borghese Gardens — about a twenty-minute walk from the Spanish Steps or fifteen from the Piazza del Popolo.
Bags must be checked at the cloakroom. No backpacks in the galleries.
Photography is allowed without flash, except in a few sensitive rooms (signposted).
The villa has two floors. Start with the ground floor for the sculpture; the painting gallery upstairs has the Caravaggios, Raphael, and Titian.
Audio guides are available for an extra fee. They're decent but not essential.
The Borghese Gardens around the villa are free and worth a separate walk — especially the Pincian Hill terrace for sunset views.
A small cafe in the gallery is overpriced. Better cafes are along Via Veneto, ten minutes' walk south.
The villa is wheelchair accessible on the ground floor; the upper floor has stairs only.
Bring water; the air conditioning is variable and the building can get warm in summer.
