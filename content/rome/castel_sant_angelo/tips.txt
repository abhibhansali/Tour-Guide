Entry is about thirteen euros. Book online to skip the ticket line.
Open daily except Mondays. Last entry is about an hour before closing.
Allow two hours; ninety minutes if you skip the inner papal apartments.
The spiral ramp is gradual but long — ninety meters of gentle uphill. Not strenuous, but worth knowing if mobility is a concern.
The top terrace can be windy and exposed; bring a layer if it's cool.
Photography is allowed everywhere except a few sensitive papal rooms.
The Ponte Sant'Angelo, the Bernini-decorated bridge in front of the castle, is worth a walk in both directions. The angels are best seen with afternoon light.
A combined ticket exists with the National Museum of Rome and some other state sites — useful only if you'll visit multiple Roman museums.
The cafe inside the castle has the best view of any cafe in Rome and the prices match that. A coffee on the upper terrace is worth one euro extra over the city below.
The Passetto di Borgo guided tour, when open, requires advance booking through the Vatican Museums. Not always available; check ahead.
The Borgo neighborhood between Saint Peter's and the castle is full of cafes and restaurants. Avoid the ones with English-only menus; the side streets have better.
