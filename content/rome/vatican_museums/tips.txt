Book online with a timed entry — same-day walk-up is brutal in peak season and may take hours.
First entry of the day, around eight, is meaningfully calmer. The 7:30 "early access" tickets are worth the premium if you can get them.
The route is essentially one-way. Decide your priorities before entering — most people regret skipping the Pinacoteca, which is off the main path.
The Sistine Chapel is the final stop. Once you exit it, you can't easily come back. Plan your bathroom break and any backtracking before the Sistine.
Strict dress code applies throughout: shoulders covered, knees covered, no hats indoors. They will stop you at the entrance if you're not compliant. Bring a light scarf or shawl if it's hot.
Large bags must be checked. Water bottles are allowed.
No flash photography anywhere. No photography at all in the Sistine Chapel — they enforce this.
The whole circuit, walked briskly, takes about two hours. Three hours is more realistic if you actually look at anything.
There's a café midway and another near the exit. Both are overpriced but functional for a sit-down break.
On the last Sunday of each month, entry is free — but the lines are extreme and the crowds are heavy. Skip unless you have nothing better planned that day.
Free water fountains are scattered along the route. Look for the small spigots in the courtyards.
