Entry is about fifteen euros. Tickets are often available same-day, but booking online avoids any queues.
The gallery is open Tuesday through Sunday; closed Mondays.
Plan two hours for a careful visit, one hour for highlights only.
The Caravaggio room is a major draw but smaller than you expect. It can get clogged at peak hours — visit early or late.
The Brera neighborhood around the palace is one of the most charming parts of central Milan. Plan to walk afterward — narrow cobbled streets, art galleries, bars, and small restaurants.
The Brera Botanical Garden, inside the same palazzo complex, is free and almost always empty. A small green oasis worth twenty minutes.
The café in the gallery is reasonable for a quick coffee but better options exist a block away.
No flash photography. Photography in general is allowed.
Audio guides are available for an extra fee. Decent but not essential — the wall labels are bilingual and detailed.
The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana — a different gallery in central Milan with a smaller but significant Renaissance collection — is a worthwhile complement if you have a second half-day in the city.
