Entry is about twenty euros. Buy online to skip ticket lines.
Open daily except Tuesdays.
Plan three hours; longer if you want to do the Secret Cabinet thoroughly.
The museum is in central Naples, a short walk from the central station or a metro stop on Line 1 (Museo station, which exits directly into the museum lobby).
Photography is allowed without flash everywhere.
Wall labels are inconsistent — some are in English, others in Italian only. An audio guide or a guidebook helps.
The cafe inside is mediocre. Better lunch options on Via Toledo or Via San Biagio dei Librai.
The mezzanine floor has the mosaics. Easy to miss because it's between the ground floor and the first floor. Look for the small stairway off the central atrium.
The Secret Cabinet is open during regular hours but is a separate room with its own small queue. Adults only; admission is included with the main ticket.
The Villa of the Papyri bronzes are on the ground floor in the same wing as the Farnese sculpture. Make sure to see both — they're often confused as one collection.
The museum is well-lit during the day but rooms can get dim in low-light afternoons. Visit earlier rather than later.
Combined tickets with Pompeii and Herculaneum exist and are good value if you'll do both.
