Entry is about thirteen euros. Combined ticket with Pompeii is around twenty-two euros and valid for three days — excellent value.
The site is open daily; closes around five p.m. in winter, seven p.m. in summer.
Plan two to three hours. The site is small enough to walk thoroughly in that time.
Wear good shoes. The ancient paving is uneven, and stairways in the houses are steep and worn.
Bring water. There is one drinking fountain near the entrance; no cafe inside.
Less shade than Pompeii but the smaller area makes summer visits more bearable. Still, mornings or late afternoons are best.
Photography is allowed; flash is discouraged in some interior rooms.
The site is reached from the Circumvesuviana railway — get off at the Ercolano Scavi stop, then walk ten minutes downhill to the site entrance.
A printed map or downloaded offline map is essential. Signage is improving but inconsistent.
Bathrooms are at the entrance and at a midway point. None are inside the ancient buildings.
Pair this with the Pompeii visit and ideally the Naples Archaeological Museum to see all three sites together.
The modern town of Ercolano outside the site has a good lunch spot called Ammodo on the main square — homemade pasta, fast service, locals' prices.
