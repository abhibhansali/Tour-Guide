Three separate tickets cover different parts of the complex. The most useful is the combined ticket for the basilica and the New Sacristy / Medici Chapels. The Laurentian Library is a separate ticket.
The basilica itself is around nine euros; New Sacristy with Chapel of the Princes is around nine euros; combined ticket is about fifteen.
The Laurentian Library is reached by a separate entrance — go through the cloister behind the basilica.
Free entry on the first Sunday of each month (state museum policy).
Strict dress code: shoulders and knees covered.
No flash photography.
Plan two hours for everything; three if you want to linger.
The Mercato Centrale food market is right next door — one of the best lunch options in Florence. The upper floor is a modern food hall, the lower floor is a traditional market.
The San Lorenzo street market outside, full of leather and souvenirs, is mostly aimed at tourists. Skip the haggling unless you're knowledgeable.
The piazza in front of the church has a statue of Giovanni delle Bande Nere, a notorious Medici condottiere from the early fifteen hundreds. He was the father of Cosimo the First.
The basilica's cloister is free and quiet — a good place to sit between visits to the different ticketed spaces.
