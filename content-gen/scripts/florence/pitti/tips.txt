The Pitti has confusing ticket combinations. The "Palatine Gallery + Royal Apartments + Boboli Gardens" combo (about twenty euros) is the best value for most visitors.
Book online for timed entry. Same-day tickets are usually available but queues can be long.
Plan a half-day for Palatine + Boboli; a full day if you also want the modern art gallery, costume gallery, and treasury.
The Palatine Gallery is hung two or three paintings deep on the walls. Take your time; major works are not always at eye level.
Boboli Gardens are huge and hilly. Wear comfortable shoes. The upper amphitheater is a steep climb but the view is worth it.
The Buontalenti Grotto, near the lower garden entrance, is open only at specific hours (usually on the hour). Worth timing your visit around.
The cafe inside the Palatine Gallery has reasonable prices for the location. The gardens have a small kiosk near the Neptune Fountain.
The Uffizi-Pitti combined ticket is valid for three consecutive days and saves money if you'll do both. Buy in advance.
Bathrooms inside the palace are limited; use the entrance ones before going up to the galleries.
The royal apartments are easier to walk through if visited after the Palatine. Many visitors skip them entirely; don't.
Boboli's main axis goes from the palace courtyard, up to the obelisk, then to the upper amphitheater. The lateral paths and lower grottoes are quieter.
