Book online weeks in advance. Same-day walk-up tickets exist but the queues can be three hours or more in peak season.
First entry of the day, around 8:15 a.m., is the only reliably uncrowded window. Reserve that slot if possible.
Allow at least three hours; four is better. The museum is much larger than it looks.
The corridor running along the Arno is best seen with afternoon light, when the windows blaze.
The Tribuna and the Caravaggio room are bottlenecks. Visit them at off-peak times or come back to them later in your visit.
The audio guide rental is decent but skippable if you have your own commentary.
Reserved seating along the corridor windows offers great rest spots without leaving the gallery.
The cafe at the rooftop terrace has the best view of Florence available from inside the museum. Worth a coffee break.
Strict bag policy: large bags must be checked at the cloakroom.
No flash photography. Tripods need a permit.
The Uffizi-Pitti combined ticket can be cost-effective if you'll also visit Palazzo Pitti. The combined ticket is valid for three consecutive days.
The bathrooms near the cafe are the cleanest in the building; the ones near the entrance get long lines.
