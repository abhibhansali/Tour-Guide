No photos, no video — they actively enforce this and will speak to you if you try.
Silence is the rule. The guards say "silenzio" every minute or two. Talk only in whispers.
Hats off indoors. Knees and shoulders covered.
Don't sit on the floor or on the steps to rest — guards will move you.
The benches around the perimeter are the best seats. They fill up fast. Get to one quickly and don't move.
Look at the ceiling from the bench near the altar end. That's where Michelangelo painted the earliest scenes — the Drunkenness of Noah is directly above. The Creation of Adam is best seen from the middle.
There are two exits. The "tour group" exit takes you directly down to Saint Peter's Basilica, saving the Saint Peter's security line. The "individual" exit sends you back to the museum entrance. If a guard waves you toward one, follow them — but if you want the shortcut to Saint Peter's, look for the door on the right of the altar.
The room gets very hot in summer. The high humidity is part of the conservation strategy, but it's uncomfortable. Plan a short visit and exit before the sweat sets in.
A pair of binoculars helps enormously for the ceiling details. The room is sixty feet tall.
Come back another day, if you can. The first visit is overwhelming. The second visit is when you actually see things.
