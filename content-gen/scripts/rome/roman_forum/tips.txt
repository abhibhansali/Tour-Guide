The Forum is included with your Colosseum ticket — same one, same twenty-four-hour window. Use the Forum entrance on Via dei Fori Imperiali, not the Colosseum entrance, for shorter security lines.
Wear shoes with grip and ankle support. The ancient paving is uneven and the inclines are steep, especially up to the Palatine.
There's very little shade. In summer, do this in the early morning or after four in the afternoon — not midday.
The Palatine Hill is included in the same ticket. The hill has the best free view in Rome, looking down into the Circus Maximus, and the imperial palace ruins on top are well worth an extra hour.
Free bathrooms and water fountains are scattered through the site — there's a good one near the entrance to the Palatine.
The Forum is mostly self-guided. A few signs are in English, more aren't. A printed map or a downloaded offline guide helps a lot — it's easy to walk past a major site without realizing what it was.
Pickpockets work the busy approach along Via dei Fori Imperiali. Once you're inside the site, it's much calmer.
Bring water and small snacks. There's a small café near the Palatine, but it's overpriced and limited.
On a hot afternoon, the shaded benches in the House of the Vestal Virgins are the best rest spot inside the whole complex.
