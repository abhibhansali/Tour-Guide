Book your timed-entry ticket online days in advance — same-day walk-up is almost impossible in peak season. The combined Colosseum-Forum-Palatine ticket is the standard one and is valid for twenty-four hours.
Arena floor access and underground access are upgrades worth the few extra euros if you can get them.
First entry slot of the day, around eight-thirty, is dramatically quieter than midday.
The line you join is not the ticket line — it's security. Skip the touts in front of the entrance trying to sell you "skip the line" tours; the real line moves quickly.
The shaded areas are almost entirely on the side facing the Forum. In summer afternoons, plan to spend more time on that side.
There's no food inside and only one small drinking fountain. Bring water; a refillable bottle is fine.
Bathrooms are at the entrance and at the upper level — nowhere in between.
Wear shoes with grip. The original travertine paving outside the building is uneven and slick when wet.
The Forum and Palatine entrance is fifty meters down the hill from the Colosseum. You can walk between them without re-queuing.
At sunset, the building lights up amber. The best free view of that is from the small terrace at the top of Via Nicola Salvi.
